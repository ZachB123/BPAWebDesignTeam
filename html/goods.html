Goods

  Consumer spending is a large sector in the total amount of greenhouse gas emissions from the world. In fact, it accounts for a whopping 26% of global greenhouse emissions. Consumer habits are driving climate change because after basic needs are met, consumers start buying items for social status. As people try to acquire more and more status, the more and more expensive status products are needed. Producing these products generates massive amounts of greenhouse gasses and it is a continuous cycle. In fact, the average product results in carbon emissions 6.3 times its own weight!

Carbon Emissions of Various Companies

  The corporations that you buy products from contribute to carbon dioxide in large amounts. However, most of these companies are taking steps to reduce their carbon footprint and signs are showing that it is working. Most companies are down millions of metric tons since a few years before 2020.

Tips/Tricks

  • Buy used instead of new
  • Less processing means less Carbon
    • Example: Frozen carrots have a CF 3 times greater than normal carrots due to the additional processing it takes to to prepare and freeze them
  • Packaging = Waste
    • Packaging involves a lot of refined plastics and other material which end up in a landfill → huge source of carbon emissions
    • Bring own bag if possible → plastic bags are not good for the environment
  • Know the Labels
    • Look for certifying agencies such as “Certified Humane Raised and Handled” instead of just claims such as “Organic” or “Cruelty-free
  • Vist the Farmers Market
    • Food from the market is fresh, seasonal, unpacked and most of the time organic
    • Avoid food-related carbon sources

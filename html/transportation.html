Transportation

  Transportation is a key factor in rising carbon emissions and climate change. In fact, 29% of total US greenhouse gas emissions come from transportation, which is the largest contributor of total emissions. Why? For most transportation such as cars, trucks and planes to operate, they burn large amounts of fossil fuels like gasoline and diesel which releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. Growing levels of carbon dioxide have generated a significant greenhouse effect which has warmed the planet at a much faster rate than expected.

Cars and trucks, being the most universal mode of transportation, contribute the most to the United States carbon footprint. Vehicle emissions have been increasing every year and this trend is unlikely to change. However, as electric vehicles and renewable energy are becoming more common, there is hope for a cleaner future.

Tips/Tricks

  • Traveling Short Distances
    • Walk or ride a bike
    • Public transportation(buses and subways) because the more people traveling in a singular vehicle the smaller the carbon footprint
    • Combine trips - run multiple errands at once or finish multiple events you have to do in one trip
  • Traveling Longer Distances
    • Trains
    • Long-distance bus
    • Replace short-flights with a car(carpool!)
  • If You Have to Fly
    • Take large commercial airliners with more people
  • Vehicle Alternatives
    • Drive an electric vehicle - they are a cleaner and greener option than gasoline as they prevent air pollution and cut fossil fuel use
    • Use fuel-efficient vehicles

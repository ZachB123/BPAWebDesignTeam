Food

    Production and transportation of food products account for 14% of total global greenhouse gas emissions. Meat alone accounts for almost 8% of total global greenhouse gas emissions. While this may not seem like a large percentage, it is quickly on the rise due to higher demand for food products and is on track to reach 20-30% of total greenhouse gas emissions in the near future. food production already contributes greatly to global warming as it produces the most methane. Methane is the main ingredient to ozone, a dangerous air pollutant that kills one million people each year.

Carbon Footprint of Different Foods

  Meats have the largest footprint out of everything else combined. This is because animals contain a special bacteria which helps them easily consume grass. Every time they consume grass, these animals emit methane which is a greenhouse gas. In contrast, veggies are extremely low on emissions as they don’t produce much methane or greenhouse gasses. Dairy also has a high carbon footprint because dairy products come from cows.

Tips/Tricks

  • Switch to a vegetarian diet
    • This is a hard switch, so switching to less carbon-intensive meats can also have a significant impact
    • Beef, for example, emits 7.2 times more greenhouse gasses per kilogram than chicken
    • Try plant-based protein: Studies show people with the lowest carbon footprint had the highest intake of plant-based protein and products
  • Stop wasting food
    • Thrown away food ends up in landfills and emits methane
  • Ditch the plastic
    • Plastic bags, wrapping, containers are used by the food industry to produce, package and transport food products
  • Cut back on dairy
    • Some products from the dairy sector (cheese) use so much milk to produce, it’s associated with a higher carbon footprint than some animal products such as pork and chicken
  • Eat more fiber-rich foods
    • Improves your digestive health, helps weight loss, balances gut bacteria, protects against serious illnesses (heart disease, cancer)
  • Grow your own produce

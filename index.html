Iowa Climate Board

Your Steps to a Greener Future Start Today

Welcome to the Iowa Climate Board

The Iowa Climate board aims to provide resources and information to reduce your carbon footprint. What is your carbon footprint you may ask? Your carbon footprint is the amount of fossil fuels you release into the atmosphere. You increase your carbon footprint by doing many activies such as heating your home, driving your car, and even buying something from a store. Our goal is to reduce individuals carbon footprints through useful tips and our handy resources such as our carbon calculator! Keep scrolling to see what the Iowa Climate Board website has to offer.

Transportation

Do you take the bus or drive your own car, or maybe you carpool. In the transportation page you will find out how each of these affects your carbon footprint and what you can do to find better alternatives.

Meat

Learn about how reducing your meat consumption or becoming vegetarian could benefit the planet. A large portion of carbon emissions is from the production of meat through deforestation, methane gas, and the shipping network used to transport the meat.

Goods

When you go to the store, do you think about how what you're buying affects the environment? Different packaging and different products have a larger carbon footprint than others. Visit the goods page to learn more about how your spending habits affect the environment.

Carbon Map

See how your carbon footprint is partially determined by your geographic location. Explore the interactive carbon map to see how carbon footprints vary around the world.

Carbon Calculator

The carbon calculator is an easy to use tool that will allow you to estimate your very own carbon footprint! All you have to do is input information about your lifestyle into the calculator and it will show you how much carbon you release every year and what you can do to reduce your footprint.

Why Care?

Learn why it is necessary to reduce our carbon footprint in order to avoid a global climate catastophe and learn how climate change will affect all of our lives.
